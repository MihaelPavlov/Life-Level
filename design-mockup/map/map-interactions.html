⚙️ Crossroads — Mechanics
Trigger
Crossroads nodes are placed at fixed map junctions. When your character reaches 2km from a fork, the "Crossroads ahead" banner appears. When you arrive, the choice modal is forced — you cannot travel further until you choose.
Path Lock
Once you choose a path it locks in. The other path grays out on the map. You can see what you missed, creating FOMO. To access the other path you must complete the chosen path first, then backtrack (costs movement).
Reward Scaling
Harder path = 2–3× XP and higher rarity item pool. Easy path = guaranteed reward. Hard path = chance at Rare/Epic but also has risks (mini boss encounter, weather debuffs, longer time window before expiry).
Path Expiry
Each path has a time limit (Easy: 5 days, Hard: 10 days). If you don't complete it in time, your character is moved back to the Crossroads and you choose again — but lose the progress already traveled.

⚙️ Dungeon Portal — Mechanics
Discovery & Entry
Portals are hidden until you pass within 3km on the map. Once discovered, they unlock permanently — you can enter from anywhere, any time, without needing to be physically at the portal location. This is the key difference from Boss Raids.
Floor Structure
Each dungeon has 3–5 sequential floors, each requiring a specific activity type: Floor 1 = running, Floor 2 = gym, Floor 3 = yoga/swim, Floor 4 (Boss) = mixed. This is the only mechanic that forces cross-training across all activity categories.
Time Limit & Reset
Dungeons have a 7–14 day timer. If you don't finish in time, the dungeon resets — you lose floor progress but keep any XP earned per floor. The portal stays visible and you can re-enter, but all floors restart from the beginning.
Boss Floor
The final floor is a "gauntlet": complete one of each activity type within 48 hours. This mimics a cross-training day. Reward is 2× if you perfect it (all 4 activities in one day). Failure returns you to the floor start, not the beginning of the dungeon.

⚙️ NPC Encounter — Mechanics
Spawn Conditions
NPCs spawn at your current map position after you log a workout. Each NPC type has a different spawn weight: Merchant (common), Quest Giver (uncommon), Mysterious Stranger (rare, gives lore + hidden zone coordinates). Only one NPC active at a time per player.
Time Window
NPCs stay for 24–48 hours then disappear. If you don't interact, you lose the encounter. This ties daily logins to map activity — you need to check the map after every workout to catch NPCs. Missing an NPC creates meaningful FOMO without frustration.
NPC Types
🧙 Merchant — sells items for XP currency (earned, not purchased). 🗡️ Quest Giver — offers a 3-day side quest with unique reward. ❓ Mysterious Stranger — gives a cryptic lore fragment + marks an undiscovered location on the map. 🛡️ Trainer — gives a 24h stat multiplier for a specific activity type.
Workout Gate
To trade or accept quests, you must have logged at least one workout that day. The NPC appears visually but interactions are greyed out with the message "Log a workout today to trade." This prevents players from passively farming NPC encounters without exercising.
