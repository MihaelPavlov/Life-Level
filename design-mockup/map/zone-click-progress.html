LEGEND & OPEN DECISIONS

Zone colour meaning (same as current code, no changes):

  • Completed — unlocked, fully explored. Green outline, check mark.
  • Active / current — where the player physically is. Blue glow + halo.
  • Destination — the zone the player is traveling toward. Orange pulsing ring, never blue-active.
  • Locked / unavailable — level too low or not adjacent. Dim grey, lock icon.

Progress formula (what drives the 62% number): distanceTraveledOnEdge / edge.distanceKm. Both numbers come from the backend. The current "always 1%" symptom suggests the backend is only ever adding ~0.1 km per activity or the edge.distanceKm is unexpectedly huge. Before wiring more UI, confirm what the backend is returning for both fields against a known activity log.

Open design decisions (resolve before implementation):

  • Tap another adjacent available zone while already traveling — three options: (a) block with "You're already traveling. Cancel current journey?" confirmation, (b) allow re-targeting but keep banked km (no reset), (c) current behavior — silently reset to 0. Mockup not yet drawn for this; pick before I sketch it.
  • Over-distance roll-over — if the user logs 20 km on a 12 km edge, does the extra 8 km bank toward the next edge automatically, or is the surplus discarded? (The board has a "Follow-up" note about this already — UserWorldProgress.PendingDistanceKm.)
  • Reserve km surfacing — should the progress card show "You have X banked km ready to spend" when there is no active destination? (Today: no. Proposed: yes, as an empty-state variant of the card.)
  • Show % on the character sprite vs. only in the sheet — the mockup shows it in both. Is that too noisy for the world-map view?
